Mathematics Laboratory (MICS), Paris-Saclay University

Research Team Member | Paris, France | Feb 2023 - Jun 2024

During my time at Paris-Saclay University, I worked on a challenging research project in collaboration with three other students under the supervision of Dr. Anna Rozanova-Pierrat. Our objective was to analyze and improve mathematical models related to acoustic wave propagation in cylindrical domains, specifically in the context of reducing jet engine noise. By utilizing a modified Myers boundary condition, we explored its effects on sound absorption and optimized the material distribution on the engine's boundary to minimize acoustic energy.

The study focused on proving the well-posedness of the problem, derived from the Euler equations, using mathematical tools such as Fredholm’s alternative, Green’s formula, and the trace operator. Additionally, we characterized physical parameters and established an optimization framework for the distribution of absorbing materials, ensuring mathematical rigor through variational formulations and measure-theoretic approaches.

This research combined theoretical and applied mathematics, with applications in aeronautics to address industrial challenges such as improving liner designs for jet engines. While the scope of the project was broad, our results contributed both to mathematical theory and practical benchmarks, culminating in a research article currently under review for publication.

Technologies used: LaTeX, Python

Concepts used: Measure Theory, Compactness, Hilbert Spaces, Fredholm’s Alternative, Variational Formulation, Trace Operator, Parametric Optimization, Acoustic Energy Minimization
